randomness

We say that something happens randomly when we cannot know in advance what is going to happen. We know that if we heat water in a pot at some point it will boil. That does not happen at random. On the other hand, if we flip a coin in the air it may come up heads or tails. We have no way of knowing which side of the coin is going to come up until we flip it.

unpredictable or uncertain?

Perhaps if we flipped the coin with a special machine and we could measure very precisely its mass and the impulse we give it we could know which side it is going to fall on. Is it a random event to flip a coin then? As long as we don't know what is going to happen we say yes.

you always know something

We will always know something. We won't know if it's going to rain in 60 days, but we do know that it will either rain, or not rain, or snow or hail. It rains, or it doesn't rain, or it snows or hails. No ice cream cones are going to fall from the sky. That's what makes using chance creatively so interesting.

long term

If we roll a die we often expect to get about the same amount of each number. But we can roll two dice and keep the smaller one or roll three and keep the sum. This way we can control the distribution that the rolls will have.
